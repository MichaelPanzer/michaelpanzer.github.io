E-Bike Conversion

Intro

For this project, I converted a vintage 2nd-hand Trek 700 Multitrack into a high-utility electric commuter. The goal was to leverage the mechanical advantages of a classic steel frame while integrating a high-torque electric drivetrain. This project involved significant mechanical fabrication, electrical system design, and custom wheel-building to handle the increased loads of an electrified system.

Frame & Power

The foundation of the build was a Trek 700 frame, chosen for its relaxed geometry and high-strength 4130 chromoly steel construction. As opposed to aluminium the 4130 will provide additional strength and fatigue resistance when subjected to the increased loads of the electric drivetrain. For propulsion, I selected a 1kW rear hub motor. This choice allowed for seamless integration into the existing frame without requiring complex mid-drive mounting brackets, maintaining the original chainline for the pedal-assist system.

Wheel Fabrication

Standard bicycle wheels are not designed for the 1kW torque spikes of a hub motor, so I laced both the front and rear wheels from scratch. Built from welded steel square tubing, the stand features adjustable forks to accommodate various hub widths and integrated mounts for dial indicators. This setup allowed me to measure and correct radial and lateral runout to within 0.1mm, ensuring a vibration-free ride at top speeds.

Safety & Integration

Engineering the electrical system required a focus on thermal management and user safety. I opted for a 48V system provide a balance between high-efficiency power delivery and electrical safety. The energy storage system was custom-built using **LG HG2 18650 cells** (3000 mAh, 20A peak), configured in a **13s4p layout**. This architecture provides a total capacity of 12 Ah and a peak current capability of 80A, comfortably exceeding the requirements of the 1kW motor.

The pack was spot-welded and housed in a standard Hailong enclosure for weather resistance and easy removal. To manage the mechanical stress of the motor's 1kW output, I installed dedicated torque arms on the rear dropouts.
